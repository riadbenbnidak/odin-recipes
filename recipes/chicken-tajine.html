Moroccan Chicken Tagine

← Back to Home

Description

Chicken Tagine with Preserved Lemon and Olives is a traditional Moroccan dish slow-cooked with fragrant spices, tender chicken, and bright flavors. Cooked in a tagine pot or a Dutch oven, this dish is known for its unique balance of savory and citrusy notes.

The preserved lemon adds a mellow tanginess, while the olives provide a salty contrast. It's typically served with warm bread or fluffy couscous to soak up the delicious sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (cut into pieces) or 6 bone-in chicken thighs
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 1 preserved lemon, sliced
  • 1 cup green or purple olives
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley or cilantro
  • 1/2 cup water or chicken broth

Steps

  1. In a large tagine or pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onions and garlic, and sauté until soft.
  2. Add the chicken pieces and brown on all sides.
  3. Sprinkle in all the spices (ginger, turmeric, cumin, paprika, cinnamon, salt, and pepper) and stir to coat the chicken.
  4. Add preserved lemon slices and olives, then pour in the water or broth.
  5. Cover and simmer gently for about 45–60 minutes, or until the chicken is tender and cooked through. Add more water if needed during cooking.
  6. Sprinkle with chopped parsley or cilantro before serving.
  7. Serve hot with couscous, rice, or fresh Moroccan bread (khobz).
