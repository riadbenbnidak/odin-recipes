Spaghetti Carbonara

← Back to Home

Description

Spaghetti Carbonara is a creamy, comforting pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and black pepper. Despite its rich taste, it’s surprisingly easy and quick to prepare, using just a few simple ingredients.

This Roman classic delivers a luxurious texture without using cream, thanks to the silky egg and cheese sauce that clings perfectly to the pasta.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz (340g) spaghetti
  • 4 oz (115g) pancetta or thick-cut bacon, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic (optional), crushed
  • Salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Boil a large pot of salted water and cook the spaghetti until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. In a pan, cook pancetta or bacon over medium heat until crispy. If using garlic, add and sauté briefly, then discard garlic.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, grated cheese, and lots of black pepper.
  4. Return the drained pasta to the pan with pancetta. Remove from heat.
  5. Quickly stir in the egg mixture, tossing vigorously. Add reserved pasta water a bit at a time until the sauce is smooth and creamy.
  6. Serve immediately, topped with extra cheese and pepper.
